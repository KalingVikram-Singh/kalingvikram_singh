I’m a final-year Integrated M.Sc. Physics student at the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER), Bhubaneswar. My academic and research interests center around condensed matter physics, with a particular focus on 2D van der Waals materials, superconductivity, and magnetism. I currently work under the supervision of Dr. Kartikeswar Senapati, where I fabricate nanostructures and study emergent quantum phenomena in low-dimensional systems. Beyond my primary research, I’m also passionate about fluid dynamics, chaos theory, and computational physics. As a Computer Science minor, I enjoy working on algorithm design (especially randomized and parameterized algorithms), numerical simulations, and applying machine learning techniques to challenges in materials science.
